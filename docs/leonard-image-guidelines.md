# Leonard Image and Sticker Guidelines

Use this document as the source of truth when creating, editing, or evaluating Leonard sticker images and website-only image assets.

## 1. Character Identity

Leonard is the **Emotional Support Fry**.

He should always feel like the same character across the sticker pack:

- Tall yellow plush fry.
- Soft fuzzy/fleece texture.
- Rounded top and rounded bottom.
- Black sunglasses.
- Small relaxed smile.
- Calm, confident, optimistic personality.
- No visible legs.
- Avoid visible arms whenever possible.

Leonard should never look anxious, confused, scared, angry, frustrated, or surprised. Even when the situation is silly, Leonard remains relaxed and confident.

## 2. Core Proportions

Leonard is based on a real plush that is approximately:

- 10 inches tall.
- 1.25 inches wide.

For most travel, destination, and environmental stickers, use Leonard’s real-world proportions:

- Tall.
- Narrow.
- Cylindrical.
- Same width at the top and bottom.
- Not pear-shaped.
- Not sausage-shaped.
- Not bean-shaped.
- Not tapered wider at the bottom.

For close-up reaction stickers, Leonard may be slightly broader if needed for readability, but he should still remain fry-like.

## 3. Face and Sunglasses

Leonard’s face is simple and consistent:

- Black wayfarer-style sunglasses.
- Small black curved smile.
- No visible eyes except when sunglasses are intentionally removed, such as sleeping stickers.
- Face should be positioned in the upper third of the body.
- Expression should remain confident and relaxed.

Avoid:
- Open mouths.
- Teeth.
- Eyebrows.
- Worried expressions.
- Confused expressions.
- Cartoon exaggeration that changes Leonard’s personality.

## 4. Limbs and Prop Handling

Leonard is a fry, not a person.

### Default Rule

Do **not** show arms, legs, feet, or full hands.

### Prop Rule

Props may float, attach, lean, or appear naturally connected without needing realistic anatomy.

This is intentional.

Acceptable examples:

- A map floating in front of Leonard.
- A backpack suspended behind Leonard with straps that do not imply arms.
- A roasting stick resting against Leonard.
- A sign held by small visible hands when needed.
- Sunglasses resting nearby in sleeping scenes.
- A blanket tucked around Leonard.
- A tiller, sailboat rigging, or marshmallow stick positioned near Leonard without showing arms.

### Hands

Small hands are allowed only when they materially improve readability.

Examples:
- Road Trip sign: small hands on top of sign are acceptable.
- Thumbs-up sticker: one visible thumbs-up hand is acceptable.

Avoid:
- Full arms.
- Long arms.
- Arms under backpack straps.
- Arms holding maps.
- Arms operating boats.
- Legs or feet.

## 5. Sticker Style

All images should look like polished iMessage stickers.

Required style:

- High-quality 3D sticker-style illustration.
- Thick white die-cut sticker border.
- Clean silhouette.
- Bright, optimistic color palette.
- Soft shadows.
- High readability at small sticker sizes.
- Transparent background preferred for final assets.
- If generated with a white background, make sure the sticker itself has a clear white outline.

Avoid:
- Photorealistic scenes that overpower Leonard.
- Busy backgrounds.
- Excessive detail in props.
- Textures or lighting that make Leonard look plastic instead of plush.
- Overly realistic mechanical detail unless essential.

## 6. Composition Rules

Each sticker should communicate one clear visual idea.

Examples:

- Road Trip = Leonard with “2026 ROAD TRIP” sign.
- Campfire = Leonard roasting a marshmallow.
- Map = Leonard with a road map.
- Arizona = Leonard with a potted cactus labeled “ARIZONA.”
- Adventure Time = Leonard with backpack and trail sign.
- Sailing = Leonard in a Sunfish-style sailboat.
- Sleeping = Leonard asleep in camper window.

Avoid combining too many ideas in one sticker. If a sticker has multiple competing concepts, split them into separate stickers.

## 7. Reaction vs. Travel Stickers

### Reaction Stickers

Leonard is the dominant subject.

Examples:
- Thumbs-Up Leonard.
- Map Leonard.
- Road Trip Leonard.

Guidelines:
- Minimal or no background.
- Props should support the reaction.
- Leonard may be slightly broader if needed.

### Travel / Environmental Stickers

Leonard exists naturally inside a setting.

Examples:
- Arizona Leonard.
- Adventure Time Leonard.
- Campfire Leonard.
- Sailing Leonard.
- Sleeping Leonard.

Guidelines:
- Use true tall/slender plush proportions.
- The environment supports the story but does not dominate.
- Backgrounds should be simplified.
- Props should be simpler than Leonard.

## 8. Backgrounds

Use backgrounds only when they help the concept.

Preferred:
- Clean white or transparent background.
- Small ground patch.
- Simple water patch.
- Camper window frame.
- Minimal landscape elements.

Avoid:
- Large photorealistic landscapes unless necessary.
- Backgrounds that compete with Leonard.
- Extra characters.
- Cluttered scenery.
- Text that is hard to read.

## 9. Text and Typography

Text should be minimal and readable at small size.

Allowed text examples:
- “2026 ROAD TRIP”
- “ADVENTURE TIME”
- “ARIZONA”

Guidelines:
- Use bold, simple lettering.
- Use signs, pots, or speech bubbles when text is necessary.
- Avoid tiny text.
- Avoid brand names or logos.
- Avoid year-specific text unless intentionally creating a year-specific sticker.

## 10. Props and Accessories

Props should be simplified and iconic.

Preferred props:
- Road signs.
- Folded paper maps.
- Marshmallow sticks.
- Campfire.
- Potted cactus.
- Backpack.
- Camper window.
- Sailboats.
- Blankets.
- Sunglasses.

Rules:
- Props should not require visible arms.
- Props should not hide Leonard’s identity.
- Props should not be more visually important than Leonard unless they are the point of the joke.
- Simplify props before adding more detail.

## 11. Sailing Sticker Rules

For Sunfish-style sailing stickers, accuracy matters.

Required:
- Small Sunfish-style sailboat.
- White hull.
- Cockpit opening aft of the mast.
- Mast deck-stepped at normal deck height.
- Deck visible on both port and starboard sides of the mast.
- Rudder attached to the transom.
- Tiller extends from the rudder toward Leonard.
- Leonard sits aft of the mast in the cockpit.
- Boom extends aft/port when running downwind.
- Boom must not point forward.
- Blank red pennant may be used for future variants.
- No brand names or logos.

Avoid:
- Mast appearing inside the cockpit.
- Deck disappearing on one side of the mast.
- Rudder floating disconnected from the stern.
- Sail or boom pointing forward in an impossible configuration.
- Excessive rigging detail.
- Life jackets that alter Leonard’s silhouette.

## 12. Website-Only Image Assets

Some generated images are only meant to support the website, not become canonical sticker-pack finals.

Before generating or adding a website image, decide its purpose:

- Sticker gallery asset.
- Homepage feature image.
- Page banner or hero image.
- Field Notes / updates image.
- Supporting documentation or process image.

Website-only assets must still follow Leonard’s character identity: tall yellow plush fry, soft texture, sunglasses, relaxed smile, calm confidence, and no unnecessary limbs.

### When The Asset Is A Sticker

Use the full sticker rules in this document.

Required:

- Thick white die-cut border.
- Clean silhouette.
- Readable at small size.
- Transparent background when possible.
- One clear concept.
- Filename under `assets/images/` using a stable, descriptive lowercase name.

Do not overwrite an existing canonical sticker file unless the new image is an approved replacement for that sticker.

### When The Asset Is Only For The Website

Website-only assets may be designed for their page context instead of iMessage use.

Allowed:

- Rectangular hero or banner composition.
- Wider scenic framing for a Field Notes page.
- Cropping room for text overlays.
- More environment than a sticker would normally use.
- No die-cut border when the asset is not intended to look like a sticker.

Still avoid:

- Extra characters.
- Busy backgrounds that bury Leonard.
- Brand names, logos, or tiny unreadable text.
- Facial expressions that make Leonard anxious, scared, angry, confused, or surprised.
- Anatomy that turns Leonard into a person instead of a fry.

### Website Asset Workflow

When adding website-only assets:

- Put files in `assets/images/`.
- Use descriptive lowercase filenames such as `leonard-campfire.png` or `leonard-heading-to-colorado-1.jpg`.
- Add or update meaningful `alt` text anywhere the image appears.
- If the image is used as a banner, check desktop and mobile crops.
- If the image is used in Field Notes, update the post front matter and inline image reference together.
- Keep source prompts and learnings in docs or issue notes when they will matter for future asset generation.
- Run `script/test-and-preview --check-only` before publishing.
- Preview the changed page locally and check for broken images, awkward crops, text overlap, and mobile overflow.

Use the canonical final stickers as character references even when generating non-sticker web art.

## 14. Evaluation Checklist

Before accepting a new Leonard sticker, verify:

- Does Leonard look like the same character?
- Is he tall, narrow, plush, and cylindrical?
- Are sunglasses and smile consistent?
- Are there any unnecessary arms, legs, or hands?
- Are props allowed to float or attach without visible limbs?
- Is there one clear visual idea?
- Is the sticker readable at iMessage size?
- Is the background simple enough?
- Is Leonard still the focal point?
- Does the white die-cut border look clean?
- Does it match the existing finalized stickers?

For website-only images, also verify:

- Is this asset clearly meant for the website rather than the sticker pack?
- Does the crop work in its actual page location?
- Does the image support the page content without overpowering it?
- Does every rendered image have meaningful alt text?

## 15. Prompt Pattern

When generating a new Leonard sticker, use a prompt structure like this:

```text
Create a high-quality 3D iMessage sticker of Leonard, the Emotional Support Fry.

Leonard is a tall, narrow, yellow plush fry with soft fleece texture, rounded top and bottom, black wayfarer sunglasses, and a small relaxed confident smile. He has no legs and should avoid visible arms or hands unless absolutely necessary. Props may float or attach naturally without requiring limbs.

Scene: [describe one clear sticker concept].

Style: polished 3D sticker illustration, bright optimistic colors, clean silhouette, thick white die-cut sticker border, transparent or clean white background, readable at small iMessage sticker size.

Avoid: extra limbs, worried expressions, busy backgrounds, brand names, confusing anatomy, and props that overpower Leonard.
```

For website-only image assets, adapt the prompt by naming the page purpose:

```text
Create a website-only image asset for Leonard, the Emotional Support Fry.

Leonard is a tall, narrow, yellow plush fry with soft fleece texture, rounded top and bottom, black wayfarer sunglasses, and a small relaxed confident smile. He has no legs and should avoid visible arms or hands unless absolutely necessary.

Website use: [homepage feature, page banner, Field Notes image, or supporting page image].

Scene: [describe one clear website image concept].

Style: warm, polished Leonard character artwork that fits the site, with enough clean composition for the intended crop. Use Leonard’s canonical proportions and personality. A die-cut sticker border is only needed if the image is meant to appear as a sticker.

Avoid: extra limbs, worried expressions, busy backgrounds, brand names, confusing anatomy, unreadable text, and props that overpower Leonard.
```

## 16. Project Philosophy

Leonard is not just a mascot. He is an emotional support fry documenting his adventures.

Every sticker should feel like another page in Leonard’s travel journal:

- Calm.
- Confident.
- Slightly absurd.
- Warm.
- Optimistic.
- Adventure-ready.
